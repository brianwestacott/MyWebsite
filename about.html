My Passions & Interests

Travel

src="images/alaska-fishing.jpg" src="images/puerto-rico-surf.jpg"

My passion for travel began at age eight during a family trip to Germany. That first international experience ignited a curiosity that hasn’t faded, leading me to explore diverse landscapes and cultures—from the rugged wilderness of Alaska and the vibrant coasts of California to the unique heritage of Puerto Rico. Travel is deeply meaningful to me because it constantly pushes me out of my comfort zone; it turns the world into a classroom where I learn how to navigate new environments and connect with people from all walks of life.

Over the years, I’ve learned to manage the logistics of independent travel, which has tested my problem-solving skills and resilience. Each trip has presented its own set of challenges, whether it was navigating transit in a foreign language or adapting to sudden changes in plans. These experiences have taught me how to remain calm under pressure, budget effectively, and think on my feet. My greatest accomplishment isn’t just the places I’ve visited, but the confidence I’ve gained in my ability to handle the unexpected.

This hobby is inseparable from my life and career goals. I view my upcoming study abroad trip to New Zealand in the fall of 2026 as the next major step in my personal and professional development. The adaptability, cultural awareness, and perspective I gain from these travels directly mirror the skills I need in a professional advisory role. Just as I approach a new city with research and an open mind, I approach client work with a focus on understanding their unique situation and finding the most effective path forward. I am eager to bring this same sense of curiosity and global mindset to my future accounting career in Duluth.

The Outdoors

src="images/outdoor-creek.jpg"

My appreciation for the outdoors began with a deep curiosity about the natural world, but it truly took root when I first experienced the silence and scale of the Boundary Waters Canoe Area Wilderness. Navigating those pristine waterways by canoe, portaging gear over rugged trails, and setting up camp under the vast Northwoods sky became an essential part of who I am. Whether I am hiking local trails or navigating the intricate labyrinth of lakes in the BWCAW, these experiences are meaningful because they demand a balance of physical endurance, careful planning, and a deep respect for the environment.

One of my most memorable achievements was a multi-day trip deep into the Boundary Waters. Facing unpredictable weather and the logistical challenge of packing only what we could carry, I learned the importance of preparation and adaptability. These trips have taught me that success in the wild—much like in business—requires foresight, a reliable team, and the ability to troubleshoot when things don't go as planned. I have faced everything from sudden storms to navigation challenges, and each experience has built my confidence and my ability to stay composed under pressure.

This hobby connects directly to my life and career goals by reinforcing my commitment to discipline and long-term planning. I have learned that the most rewarding destinations often require the most significant effort, a lesson I apply to my academic studies and professional goals in accounting. As I look toward my career in Duluth, I value the opportunity to live in a region that balances professional ambition with such incredible access to the outdoors. The patience, resilience , and problem-solving skills I’ve gained from the wilderness are the same qualities I intend to bring to my professional engagements, ensuring that I approach every client’s challenge with a clear, calm, and prepared mindset.
